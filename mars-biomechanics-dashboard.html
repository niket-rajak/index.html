At 0.38g, preferred walking speed decreases by ~20%, stride length increases by ~37%, and cadence drops by ~32%. The extended aerial phase (ballistic flight between steps) is a key biomechanical marker of Martian locomotion, resembling a slow-motion run. Ground reaction forces are reduced proportionally to gravity.

Bone mineral density on Mars decreases from 100% at mission start to approximately 88.6% by month 12, losing roughly 0.22% per week. Earth baseline remains flat at 100%.
Based on ISS data extrapolated to 0.38g partial gravity. Actual loss rate may vary with exercise countermeasures (ARED resistance training reduces loss to ~0.5%/month). WHO osteoporosis threshold is T-score ≤ −2.5, corresponding to approximately 87–88% of peak BMD. Without countermeasures, a 12-month Mars mission approaches clinically significant bone loss.
